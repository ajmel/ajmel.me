Electric Longboard

Pressure sensitive electric longboard that rides as the user leans forwards or backwards.

Role

  • Power Electronics
  • Arduino programming

This project was part of the MIT Edgerton Center Engineering Design Workshop (EDW), a month long program where high-school students come together and form groups to work on a project. My team decided to work on an electric longboard that was controlled by the rider by leaning forwards or backwards. The longboard has two custom-wound (by an MIT graduate student) brushless 3-phase DC motors, two 4s1p 26.4V, 2Ah LiFe batteries , 2 Kelly KBS motor controllers, an Arduino Uno, and two pressure sensors. As the rider steps on the front and rear pressure sensors, they change the input voltage into the Arduino. Comparing the relative magnitude of these voltages allows the longboard to determine if it should accelerate or if it should brake. The longboard has a maximum recorded speed of 15mph and a range of around 8miles.

Responsible for power electronics wiring, including motor controller and batteries and for writing code that would allow the rider to control the speed of the skateboard by inclining forward or backward.
