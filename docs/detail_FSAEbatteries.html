FSAE Battery Team

Development of a a Safe, High-Performance, Cylindrical Cell Lithium-Ion Battery Pack for an Electric Racecar. MIT Luis de Florez Design competition 1st place.

Responsibilities

  • Mechanical Engineer
  • Design for assembly, safety, and reliability

Custom battery pack using 18650 Li-Ion Cylindrical Cells, that is lighter, safer and contains more energy than the battery pack that was used two years ago to compete in FSAE. Pack consists of 6 modules, each of which is 12s12p for a total amount of energy of 7kWh (25MJ), about 1/10 that of a Tesla Model S. System level energy density is 100Wh/kg (360kJ/kg). The pack operates at a voltage of 300V, which made safety and ease of servicing our top priority.

Responsible for designing and making a fully serviceable, electrocution safe enclosure for the batteries HV electronics.

The design for the HV electronics was based heavily on safety, and ease of serviceability. Previous year's pack was extremely dangerous to handle since it was easy for someone to accidentally electrocute themselves. In addition, the reduction in part count through the use of PEM Engineering fasteners aided not only with assembly but also minimized the chances of a metal part accidentally causing a short circuit.

The HV electronics were placed in order to minimize the number of steps required to disassemble a given component. This was achieve by placing more permanent components towards the back and placing more used or replaced components towards the front. The assembly contains a bottom layer that contains all of the HV electronics while the top contains all other components that interface with HV that are LV themselves. Layers of FR4 protect someone from accidentally electrocuting themselves.

Each module was made from custom machined polycarbonate holders for each of the cells. All of the cells were then glued onto the plates and let dry. The connections between the cells was done by placing the nickel strip on the cells and spot welding them. The polycarbonate plates were designed so that there is only one possible way of placing the nickel strips, to prevent accidental shorting.

The pack has a custom Battery Management System (BMS) that keeps track of all cell voltages to prevent an under voltage fault.

Each cell is individually fused through the use of 0.005" custom nickel-plated steel strips. Each module has 6 thermocouples in either side, which are monitored by the BMS to ensure that the cell operating temperature never exceeds 60C.

300W worth of fans cool down the cells, and the pack's peak operating power was around 60kW.
