MIT 2.007 Design & Manufacturing Robot

Designed and manufactured a remote-controlled robot to compete in a Star-Wars themed game table by performing tasks such as spinning and pulling objects.

Role

  • Front-end Design
  • Branding
  • Information Architecture
  • Data Gathering

MIT's 2.007: Design and Manufacturing is a class where students ideate, design, prototype and validate a robot that can perform different tasks such as pushing, pulling or spinning objects in a game table to score points. There are different ways of scoring points by performing the aforementioned tasks, and it is up to the students to come up with a strategy for success. Students can score points by moving Stormtroopers, spinning the X-Wing thrusters and tilting the lightsaber to multiply the number of points obtained.

My robot is made up of two different parts, one responsible for spinning the thrusters and the other responsible for pulling the lightsaber. After prototyping, the robot is mainly waterjet-cut aluminum with turned spacers and adapters in order to make it easy to assemble and disassemble.

My 2.007 robot. In the front, the robot is able to spin the thrusters by engaging the two prongs. On the rest of the robot, a two-degree of freedom crane comprised of a rack and pinion mechanism and a winch, is able to tilt the lightsaber to multiply the amount of points.

The spinning mechanism involves a motor attached to a lasercut adapter piece that connects it to the two prongs machined from Delrin on the lathe.

The robot made it to the top 16 at the competition, out of a class of almost 150 students.
