Aurora Monkeyalis

Travelled to Beijing, China to teach a class on engineering, art, and science to over 100 students through the creation of an RGB night-lamp.

Responsibilities

  • Design
  • Teaching

As part of the MIT Edgerton Center outreach, I travelled to Beijing, China with my colleague and two MIT alums to teach students about engineering, art, and science. Traditionally, one of the Center's workshop for students to learn about electronics and soldering is to create a night lamp made from laser-cut wood, 3 LEDs, a resistor, a switch, and a battery. We wanted to host this workshop in Beijing, but since there were going to be more than 100 students and they were all in middle school, we did not want to use soldering irons to prevent them from injuring themselves. This is why my colleague and I were given the task of designing a version that was solderless, but still achieved the same functionality.

It is called the Aurora Monkeyalis because with the use of 3 colored LEDs and a dark room, the light projects from the acrylic onto the ceiling, creating the effect of the Aurora Borealis.

First iteration made out of foam and hot-glue

We decided to make the electrical connections by twisting the component leads using pliers. We used laser-cut acrylic monkeys because it was year of the monkey in China!

Late night preparation of 100+ kits for the students.
