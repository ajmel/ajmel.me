MIT Formula SAE

This semester I joined the MIT Formula SAE as a Mechanical Engineer in the battery pack subteam. This is the first year we are making our own battery pack.

We are using Samsung 18650 cylindrical cells (similar to those used at Tesla). We are planing on having six 12s12p modules for a total of around 4.5kWh of energy. We have designed everything from the cell holders, to bus-bars to the enclosure that will hold all of the modules and other HV electronics.

My work involves designing the HV eletronics packing for the accumulator. It is required to be two-finger safe, fully serviceable and capable of delivering 15kW of power without failure.

The image above shows the current CAD model for the entire battery pack (not including the lid). The pack was made trapezoidal to allow the entire frame of the car to be moved back. This improves our rear-weight bias and allows us to gain greater accelerations.
